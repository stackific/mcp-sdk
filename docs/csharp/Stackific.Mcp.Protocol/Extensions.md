## Remarks

S11 owns the lexical layer: the extension-identifier grammar (`prefix/name`, with a REQUIRED prefix — distinct from the OPTIONAL-prefix `_meta`-key grammar in [`MetaKeys`](../Stackific.Mcp.Json/MetaKeys.md)), the `extensions` map / settings-object shapes, normalization (dropping `null`/malformed entries per R-6.5-j), the activation-by-intersection primitive, and the forward-compatibility helpers. S38 builds the mechanism on top: the third-party reservation policy including the bare-token prohibition, per-request active-set computation, the four sanctioned surface channels, method/notification namespacing, controlled reserved `_meta` keys, the open `resultType` set, versioning, graceful degradation, the declarative [`ExtensionDefinition`](./Extensions.ExtensionDefinition.md) validator, and the active-set-gated [`ExtensionMethodRouter`](./ExtensionMethodRouter.md).

Raw `extensions` maps are modeled here as JsonObject (the wire shape: a map from extension identifier to a settings object), because forward compatibility requires tolerating arbitrary (possibly malformed: `null`, array, scalar) values without rejecting the whole map. A receiver normalizes such a map with [`NormalizeMap`](./Extensions/NormalizeMap.md).

Cross-file integration note.`Protocol/Capabilities.cs` currently exposes `HasExtension(id)` as a bare `ContainsKey` over its raw extensions dictionary, which treats a `null`- or array-valued entry as advertised — a conformance bug versus R-6.5-j. It SHOULD route advertisement/intersection checks through [`IsAdvertised`](./Extensions/IsAdvertised.md), [`NormalizeMap`](./Extensions/NormalizeMap.md), and [`Intersect`](./Extensions/Intersect.md) defined here.
